<p><p>The ceramic filtering membrane market is categorized into three main types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration membranes are designed to remove larger particles, such as bacteria and suspended solids, while ultrafiltration membranes target smaller particles including viruses and colloids. Nanofiltration membranes fall between these two, effectively removing small organic molecules and divalent ions. Each type serves specific applications in water treatment, food processing, and pharmaceuticals, highlighting the versatility and efficiency of ceramic membranes in filtration technologies.</p></p>
