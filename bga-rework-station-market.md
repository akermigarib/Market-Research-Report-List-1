<p><p>BGA rework stations are vital for modifying electronic components, particularly Ball Grid Array (BGA) chips. The market typically features two primary types: hot air and infrared (IR) rework stations. Hot air stations utilize heated air to evenly distribute heat, enabling precise control during soldering and desoldering. In contrast, infrared stations employ radiant heat from infrared lamps, allowing for rapid, localized heating, which minimizes damage to surrounding components. Both types cater to different rework needs and preferences in precision and efficiency.</p></p>
