Conventional vaccines are used for routine immunization in livestock to create herd immunity, typically requiring multiple doses to maintain efficacy. Emergency vaccines, on the other hand, are designed for rapid deployment during outbreaks to quickly control the spread of the disease, often allowing for single-dose administration or faster production. Both types play crucial roles in safeguarding livestock health and agricultural economies.</p></p>
