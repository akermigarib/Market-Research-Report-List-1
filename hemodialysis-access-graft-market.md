<p><p>The hemodialysis access graft market comprises three primary types: biological grafts, synthetic grafts, and biosynthetic grafts. Biological grafts use natural tissue, promoting enhanced integration with the body but may face limits in availability and longevity. Synthetic grafts, made from materials like polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), offer durability and consistency but can be prone to complications. Biosynthetic grafts blend biological and synthetic components, aiming to improve biocompatibility while retaining the strength of synthetic materials, addressing the limitations of the other types.</p></p>
