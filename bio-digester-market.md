<p><p>The bio-digester market is primarily divided into two types: dry fermentation and wet fermentation. Dry fermentation involves the digestion of organic materials with low moisture content, promoting efficient biogas production while minimizing water usage. It's often used for solid biomass, such as agricultural residues. In contrast, wet fermentation processes utilize high-moisture organic materials, like food waste and slurry, favoring more extensive microbial activity. This method typically requires larger facilities but can process diverse feedstocks with higher biogas yields.</p></p>
