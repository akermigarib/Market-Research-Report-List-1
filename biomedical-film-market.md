<p><p>The biomedical film market encompasses various polymer types, each serving specific applications. PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene) offers chemical resistance, while PVDF (polyvinylidene fluoride) is known for its durability and biocompatibility. PP (polypropylene) is lightweight and cost-effective. PSU (polysulfone) and PESU (polyethersulfone) are chosen for their thermal stability and strength. TPU (thermoplastic polyurethane) offers flexibility and resilience. Other materials in the market may include nylon and PET, catering to diverse biomedical uses such as packaging, surgical drapes, and medical device components.</p></p>
