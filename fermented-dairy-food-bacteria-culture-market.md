<p><p>The fermented dairy food bacteria culture market is divided into two main types: mesophilic and thermophilic bacteria. Mesophilic bacteria thrive at moderate temperatures (20-45°C) and are commonly used in cheeses like cheddar and cottage cheese, as well as in yogurt production. In contrast, thermophilic bacteria prefer higher temperatures (45-75°C) and are essential for making products such as Greek yogurt and certain cheeses like mozzarella. Each type plays a crucial role in determining the texture, flavor, and quality of fermented dairy products.</p></p>
